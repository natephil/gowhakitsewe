verbs

Gowhakits'eewe has a complex tense system that is rich with many different ways to express how, when, and where you received the knowledge you are sharing.

Verbal inflection is rich with a large number of tenses. Tense is usually carried by a suffix that attaches to the verb. The tense suffix may signal recent past, intermediate past, the long-ago-but-remembered past, the distant past, the intermediate future, or the distant future. For example, the suffix -testing indicates that the verb describes an event that took place in the recent past, usually earlier the previous day as seen in the Gowhakits'eewe sentence, "testing" (testing).

Nouns

I am working towards a goal of having most of my nouns be derived from verbs. This means nouns are highly under developed but will be easy to quickly build up after verbs. The plan is to come up with a set of nominalizers that will affix to the word of interest to make a noun. The phonology of the affix will depend on the class of noun you are trying to create which is motivated largely by size and sentience of the object (there will be allophony in the nominalizers so phonological variation in the class affixes is to be expected.).

Posession in Gowhakits'eewe is typically shown with vowel lengthening within affixes. Whether it is a prefix or a suffix depends on person, tense agreement, transitivity, etc.

Number System

I haven't really started on fleshing out a number system yet. I feel it's too early because it could be affected by many sensorimotor interactions with the natives' world.

My current idea is that they have a base twenty system. They are a race of people descended from humans but have lived on their own planet isolated for thousands of years and have evolved to better fit their environment. As a result, they have tougher bottoms of their feet and no longer need shoes. They not only count with their fingers but they count with their toes as well giving them a base twenty system.

In addition to this, they are a nomadic people and the only race of humans on this planet. They live in close proximity to eachother at all times and often do tasks together. For numbers above 20 you start with, "first digit of second person." For numbers over 40 you start with, "first digit of third person." Etc. There is no distinction between the words "fingers" and "toes".
